Four Critical Barriers

Indonesia's Diagnostic & AI Healthcare Gap

Despite significant investments in healthcare digitization through SATUSEHAT, four interconnected barriers block Indonesia from building a truly effective, equitable, and AI-ready healthcare system.

BARRIER 01

Missing Standards & Quality Benchmarks

  • No nationally unified diagnostic testing standards ‚Üí AI solutions built on foreign protocols don't reflect Indonesian patient populations
  • No localized clinical pathways for Indonesia-specific diseases (TB, dengue, maternal health, diabetes)
  • No quality benchmarks for AI model validation ‚Üí no way to certify which AI tools are safe and effective
  • Without standardization, quality control data is meaningless and interoperability impossible
BARRIER 02

Fragmented, Non-Interoperable Data

  • Medical data scattered across 5,000+ facilities in incompatible formats
  • Imaging archives disconnected from laboratory results ‚Üí multi-modal AI remains impossible
  • Patient histories split between public hospitals, private clinics, and screening programs
  • Inconsistent coding practices ‚Üí data aggregation across institutions becomes unreliable
BARRIER 03

No Commercialization or Regulatory Pathway

  • No clear AI certification pathway through BPOM ‚Üí promising solutions stuck in perpetual "pilot" status
  • No reimbursement codes for AI-assisted services ‚Üí hospitals cannot justify procurement costs
  • No connection between innovators and payers ‚Üí no sustainable business model for AI developers
  • International solutions can't enter; local solutions can't scale ‚Äî a market with no exit
BARRIER 04

Weak Lab Infrastructure & Regional Diagnostic Inequality

  • No tiered Reference Laboratory framework ‚Üí complex cases have no defined escalation path
  • POCT devices at primary level operate in isolation with no central quality oversight or anomaly detection
  • Diagnostic quality gap between urban hospitals and rural Puskesmas creates inequitable patient outcomes
  • Redundant repeat testing during referrals wastes resources and delays clinical decisions

These four barriers are interconnected. Without lab infrastructure, data cannot be standardized. Without standardization, AI cannot be validated. Without validation, there is no regulatory pathway. Without a pathway, there is no sustainable ecosystem. The solution must address all four simultaneously.

IVD Consumables Manufacturing ‚Äî Indonesia Supply Chain Initiative

Building a sovereign in-country supply chain to sustain Indonesia's primary healthcare screening programs at scale

Strategic Imperative

To fully deliver on Indonesia's Primary Healthcare Strengthening Program and Free Health Screening Program, diagnostic services must be underpinned by a reliable, cost-efficient, and locally anchored IVD consumables supply chain. Dependence on imported reagents creates cost volatility, procurement delays, and quality risks ‚Äî especially at primary care level where volume is highest and supply continuity is most critical. An in-country manufacturing capability is not a back-office logistics concern; it is a strategic prerequisite for sustainable national health coverage.

In-Country IVD Manufacturing Plan

Local IVD Consumables Factory
Plan to co-build an Indonesia-based GMP-certified manufacturing facility for IVD reagents and POCT consumables with strategic partners ‚Äî targeting domestic supply self-sufficiency and BPOM regulatory compliance from day one
Product Focus
POCT test strips & cartridges for NCD screening (diabetes / HbA1c, hypertension, lipid panels), infectious disease rapid tests (TB, dengue, HIV), maternal health panels, newborn screening, and QC reference materials for the national EQA network
Partnership Model
Co-investment with UBC Medical Indonesia and Diastika Biotekindo ‚Äî leveraging their government distribution networks, BPOM regulatory relationships, and deep knowledge of local clinical demand patterns across 34 provinces

How the NRL Infrastructure Enables This

Reference Lab as Quality Anchor
The NRL provides authoritative method validation, lot release testing, and reference standard traceability for all locally manufactured consumables ‚Äî ensuring every production batch meets the same national diagnostic standards as reference-level testing
Unified LIS/LIMS as Demand Signal
Real-time usage data from 5,000+ connected facilities flows through the unified LIS/LIMS ‚Äî enabling precise demand forecasting, reducing waste, optimizing production cycles, and ensuring uninterrupted supply to Primary Care Screening Points
National POCT QC Network as Post-Market Monitor
The centralized QC network continuously monitors in-field performance of deployed consumables at all Primary Care Screening Points ‚Äî providing real-world post-market surveillance data and rapid quality feedback loops to the factory
Cost Reduction
Local production lowers per-test consumable cost, making mass screening economically viable at national scale
Supply Security
In-country production insulates Indonesia from global supply chain disruptions and import dependency
NRL-Validated Quality
